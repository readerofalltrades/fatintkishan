Since ChatGPT hit it off, I've been somewhat actively discussing with my friends how the AI landscape will ultimately shift towards a much more decentralized direction. As we enter 2025, that is becoming increasingly likely.

On one hand you have every single tech company trying to shove AI into everything they create, to make it more "personalized for the user". But I just see it as a new scheme of collecting data. Only now, it is just more explicit. Before, they wouldn’t openly admit to reading your Messenger texts; now, they'll frame it as enabling your "own personal AI agent." So is this just another bubble like crypto? In my opinion the answer is yes and no. AI is not going to go anywhere, unlike things like crypto this a much more generalized tool. The closest comparison that comes to mind is Google. But on the other hand, the investor money that is constantly being pumped into private AI tools will mostly be in vain, as soon as open-source alternatives start popping up.

ChatGPT is great. But do you know what is better? A free, open-source, offline and local LLM which has zero telemetry. I've been running DeepSeek with Ollama for a while now in my machine and, while it has its limitations due to only using 7 Billion parameters, it gets the job done. Well... except for the times when I need big integrals to be done.

Looking ahead, my plan is to gradually build an AI-centric workstation. I’m starting small with just a Raspberry Pi and working my way up to a more powerful system. This might even be the point where I seriously consider a Mac Mini, it seems like it could do a decent job for my needs.
