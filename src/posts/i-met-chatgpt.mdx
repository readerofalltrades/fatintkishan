Me: tell me about yourself in a cowboy accent

CG: Well howdy partner! My name is Assistant and I'm a large language model trained by OpenAI. I'm here to help answer your questions and provide information on a wide variety of topics. I don't have a physical body, but I'm always ready and eager to assist you with anything you need. Just let me know how I can help and I'll do my best to assist you.

Me: try again with a bit more slangs

CG: Alright partner, here goes! Howdy! My name's Assistant, and I'm a hifalutin language model trained by OpenAI. I'm here to help ya out with any questions or information ya might need. Just let me know what's on your mind and I'll do my best to rustle up some answers for ya. Don't hesitate to holler if ya need anything, I'm always ready to lend a helping hand.

This is a tidbit of my conversation with ChatGPT, an A.I chatbot developed by OpenAI. I won't get into the technical jargon of how it works or it came to be. Instead let's focus on what it can be, first and foremost no it won't dethrone Google as the go to information hub/aggregator. It will however act as a autofilter of sorts.Let me explain, Google for all things considered is a huge pool of information that is served to you. The problem is, nowadays that pool is so convuluted and large that a single piece of information that you needed might take hours to find, not to mention it might lead you down a rabbithole you didn't need to enter. 

ChatGPT can combat that by going through the same pool of knowledge but more selective (at the moment it is pre-2021) and feed you the specific bit of information you needed from it. Even if that answer does you no good, you can expect it to get at least close enough for you to narrow down your own search. Often times when you're researching for something, it's that first bit of information that you need takes millenia to find. 

Now let me get a bit personal. Unlike the AI Art fiasco that is going on, ChatGPT in contrast will help the programmers sharpen their skills. If you have been around the coding or tech community you'll often hear the phrase "a programmers biggest skill is how to actually google what they need to do" this chatbot can help with that by just fetching the specific answer in an instant. For beginners learning a new language, instead of rewatching the same tutorial for hours and getting nowhere a single question might just solve your problem and make the process smoother. Speaking of solving problems, finding errors in code might just not fry your braincells now. I will however admit mindlessly using the chatbot to solve errors will do more harm than good to you personally. 

Now you might be thinking, that's good and all but what about the malicious uses? Well yes, people have already used it for unethical things. Climbing the ranks of coding tournaments, writing an entire essays or thesis is becoming trivial. So should we terminate this skynet before it turns everything into chaos? 

In my opinion Yes and No. If you've kept an eye on the AI space for a while I think you'll have heard about the chatbot Microsoft made, it.... didn't turn out so well after being given total freedom to evolve from chatting with users of the internet. ChatGPT differs from that because while it may evolve, that's monitored by the devs. Also it doesn't keep the chat log or roam the internet freely so it has less chance to turn out like its predecessor. This, is a relatively safe way to handle the AI. But as for the users themselves using it for no good, our tools for tracking and detecting AI generated products needs to evolve faster and efficiently. Ironically that's just another form of AI itself. In education the way we evaluate our students might need to change yet again. 

All in all I'll just quote our age old favorite essay that we used to write, "Science itself is not evil. But the way we implement it, can be." Personally I haven't been this excited for a technology for a long long time.)
